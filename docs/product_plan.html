Product Strategy · Full Plan · English

JobUFO — All-in-One Job Application OS

Resume builder + ATS checker + auto-fill + application tracker + Sankey analytics. One platform. The entire job-hunting workflow, covered.
01 · Brainstorm
The core insight: job seekers use 5 tools to do 1 thing
Right now a job seeker has to juggle LinkedIn (find jobs), Resume.io (write resume), Huntr/Trello (track applications), ChatGPT (polish copy), and Excel (log data). Information is scattered, workflow is broken. JobUFO's value is stitching the whole chain together in one place.
Pain 1 — no feedback on resume qualityATS filters out 85% of resumes. Candidates have no idea why they're being screened out.
Pain 2 — re-typing the same info constantlyEvery new application means filling in name, education, and work history from scratch. Tedious and error-prone.
Pain 3 — no visibility on application outcomesHow many sent? Who replied? What's the conversion rate? Zero data available.
Pain 4 — can't tell which channel is workingSEEK vs LinkedIn vs direct — job seekers have no way to compare performance across channels.
Opportunity — AI is barely used in job toolsExisting products have shallow AI integration. JD matching and keyword optimisation are wide open.
Opportunity — Australian market gapDeep SEEK/GradConnection integration, local visa context. Global products don't bother.

03 · Product features
Three core modules
1

Resume builder + ATS score

Drag-and-drop resume editor with clean templates. AI analyses keyword density, formatting compliance, and ATS friendliness in real time — giving a 0–100 score plus specific, actionable feedback (e.g. "Missing 'data analysis' — suggest adding to paragraph 2"). Generate a tailored version for each JD.

2

Smart auto-fill profile

User fills in a complete "job profile" once (education, experience, skills, visa status). Install the browser extension, then apply on SEEK/LinkedIn/company sites and one click auto-populates every field. Supports multiple resume versions and remembers field mappings per platform.

3

Application tracker + Sankey analytics

Every application is logged automatically (or added manually): company, role, channel, date, status. Track the full funnel: Applied → Test → Phone screen → Interview → Final → Offer/Rejected. A Sankey diagram shows conversion rates at a glance, helping users double down on what's working.

07 · Competitive moat
Why users will stick around

Data flywheel

The more applications a user logs, the more personalised and valuable the analytics become. Historical data is hard to migrate away from.

Auto-fill habit lock-in

Once the Chrome extension is installed and muscle memory kicks in, switching cost is high. Uninstalling feels like a step backwards.

Australian localisation depth

Deep SEEK and GradConnection integration, Australian visa status fields, local salary benchmarks. Global competitors simply don't invest here.

Community + UGC

Users can anonymously share their Sankey diagrams ("I sent 200 applications — here's my funnel"). Social sharing drives organic growth.

08 · Risks and mitigations
High

Teal or LinkedIn copies the feature set

Mitigation: stay ahead with Australian localisation and fast iteration. Large companies move slowly. Build community moat early.

High

SEEK / LinkedIn anti-bot measures block the extension

Mitigation: use DOM manipulation (not scraping), stay within ToS. Research platform policies early. Build a manual-import fallback.

Medium

High churn — users cancel once they land a job

Mitigation: also target career-changers, not just fresh grads. B2B revenue offsets C2C seasonal dips.

Medium

Claude API costs rise as scale grows

Mitigation: cap ATS checks on the free tier, cache common JD analyses, unlimited usage only for Pro subscribers.

Low

Building solo is slow

Mitigation: validate demand with a no-code prototype first (Webflow + Notion). Only go heads-down on full dev once someone tries to pay you.

Your next move

Solo build — first validate the core assumption: will people actually pay for an ATS check?
